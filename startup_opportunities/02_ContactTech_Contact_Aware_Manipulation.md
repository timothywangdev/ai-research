## üìä Market Analysis

### The Pain (40x Underrepresentation)

**Finding from 800 Papers**:
- **90% of real manipulation is contact-rich**:
  - Insertion (peg-in-hole, USB, connectors)
  - Assembly (parts fit together)
  - Screwing/fastening (torque sensing)
  - Tool use (hammering, wiping, cutting)
  - Grasping fragile objects (force control)

- **Only 2.5% of papers model contact** (20 out of 800)
  - Vision-based methods dominate (600 papers, 75%)
  - Contact explicitly modeled: 20 papers (2.5%)
  - **40x underrepresentation**

**Why This Matters**:
- Lab demos: Pick-and-place works (no contact)
- Real world: Assembly fails (contact-critical)
- **Industry pain**: "Works in lab, fails in factory"

### Who's Suffering

**1. Manufacturing (Primary Market)**
- **Automotive**: 5M assembly operations/day (connectors, bolts, panels)
- **Electronics**: PCB assembly, connector insertion (sub-mm tolerances)
- **Aerospace**: High-precision assembly (tight tolerances, high forces)
- **Pain**: Human assembly costs $30-50/hour, error-prone, labor shortage
- **TAM**: $35B (assembly automation in manufacturing)

**2. Medical Devices**
- **Surgical robotics**: Force feedback critical (don't damage tissue)
- **Device assembly**: Sterile environments, precision required
- **TAM**: $5B (surgical + device assembly)

**3. Construction**
- **Robotic assembly**: Modular building, 3D printing with force control
- **TAM**: $3B (automated construction)

**4. Consumer Electronics**
- **Phone assembly**: Tiny connectors, precise force needed
- **TAM**: $2B (electronics assembly automation)

**Total Addressable**: $45B across sectors
