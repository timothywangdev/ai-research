## üéì KEY LEARNINGS FOR INVESTORS

### What's Actually Getting Funded (2024-2025 Pattern)

**FUNDABLE**:
1. ‚úÖ Humanoid robots with enterprise customers (Figure, Apptronik)
2. ‚úÖ Foundation models with massive data advantage (Physical Intelligence, Skild)
3. ‚úÖ Warehouse automation with proven ROI (Dexterity, Pickle)
4. ‚úÖ Niche solutions with OEM partnerships (Collaborative Robotics)

**NOT FUNDABLE**:
1. ‚ùå SMB-focused robotics (market doesn't exist)
2. ‚ùå Autonomous RL approaches (15-year failure)
3. ‚ùå No-code for novices (abstraction wrong)
4. ‚ùå General-purpose without differentiation (too broad)

### Investor Preferences Observed

**What VCs Want to See**:
1. **Team**: Ex-Google, CMU PhDs, Amazon robotics veterans
2. **Traction**: Paying pilots (not free), LOIs from Fortune 500
3. **Differentiation**: 10-100x advantage (not 2x)
4. **Capital Efficiency**: Path to $10M ARR on $5M seed
5. **Exit Path**: Strategic buyers identified (Amazon, Google, OEMs)

**What VCs Avoid**:
1. **First-time founders** without robotics background
2. **Research projects** without productization plan
3. **Broad TAM** without niche focus
4. **Long sales cycles** without capital efficiency plan
5. **Unproven tech** (composition, autonomous RL, etc.)
