## üéì TOP 10 LEARNINGS

### What VCs Taught Us (The Hard Way)

**1. Google/OpenAI Are Ahead, Not Behind**
- Assumption: "18-month window before they notice"
- Reality: They're 12-18 months AHEAD already
- Lesson: Don't compete head-on with $100B companies

**2. RT-X Data Is NOT Accessible**
- Assumption: "RT-X is open-source, freely usable"
- Reality: $500K-2M + 12 months + Google approval for commercial use
- Lesson: Verify data licensing before building entire strategy on it

**3. Compute Costs Underestimated 10-100x**
- Assumption: "$400K for foundation model training"
- Reality: $5-10M for pre-training, $50K-500K/day for RL at scale
- Lesson: Talk to people who've actually trained models

**4. SMB Robotics Market Is a Graveyard**
- Assumption: "90% can't afford engineers = huge market"
- Reality: They can't afford $200K robots either (Rethink, Fetch failed)
- Lesson: Historical evidence > theoretical TAM

**5. RL Has 15-Year Failure Track Record**
- Assumption: "RL + diffusion hybrid will work"
- Reality: 100+ startups, $1B+, ZERO products in 15 years
- Lesson: Don't be the 101st to try the same failed approach

**6. OEMs Won't Buy From External Vendors**
- Assumption: "OEMs will buy universal AI layers"
- Reality: Tesla (300+), ABB (100+), UR (50+) AI engineers in-house
- Lesson: Selling to people who build robots = wrong customer

**7. Unproven Research ‚â† Products**
- Assumption: "Composition learning papers ‚Üí fundable startup"
- Reality: Works in labs, fails in production (2-5x vs 1,000x claimed)
- Lesson: Deployed systems > promising papers

**8. Enterprise Sales Cycles Kill Startups**
- Assumption: "Enterprise will buy quickly"
- Reality: 18-24 month cycles, burn $5-8M before first revenue
- Lesson: Revenue Year 1 or die in "valley of death"

**9. Exit Expectations Were 5-10x Too High**
- Assumption: "$5-20B exits possible"
- Reality: $300-500M more realistic for niches
- Lesson: Comparable companies (Covariant, Skild) = $1-3B max

**10. Niche Wins, Broad Loses**
- Assumption: "All robots, all tasks = bigger TAM"
- Reality: "UR5 picking" is fundable, "all robotics" is not
- Lesson: 1,000 customers in niche > 10 in broad market

## üö´ WHAT NOT TO DO

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

**1. Don't Pivot Back to "All Robotics"**
- Temptation: "We can expand to other robots easily"
- Reality: Lose focus, dilute resources, fail to prove niche
- Discipline: Stay focused on UR5 + picking until $10M ARR

**2. Don't Assume RT-X Is Accessible**
- Temptation: "We'll just use RT-X data"
- Reality: $500K-2M + 12 months licensing
- Alternative: Build your own dataset from customer deployments

**3. Don't Compete with Google/OpenAI**
- Temptation: "We can be faster"
- Reality: They're ahead + have 100x your resources
- Strategy: Find niches they ignore

**4. Don't Target SMBs**
- Temptation: "90% of market can't afford engineers"
- Reality: They can't afford robots either
- Focus: Mid-market (100-500 employees) + large enterprises

**5. Don't Bet on Unproven Research**
- Temptation: "Composition will give us 1,000x"
- Reality: It doesn't work in production
- Stick to: Proven technologies (equivariance, transfer)

**6. Don't Underfund**
- Temptation: "We can do it with $1-2M pre-seed"
- Reality: 18-24 month sales cycles, need $5M minimum
- Raise: $5M seed (not $1-2M pre-seed)

**7. Don't Overpromise Exits**
- Temptation: "We'll be a $5B company"
- Reality: $300-500M is excellent outcome
- Investors: Prefer realistic vs fantasy

**8. Don't Skip Customer Validation**
- Temptation: "Build it and they will come"
- Reality: 3 LOIs required before funding
- Process: Customer discovery ‚Üí LOIs ‚Üí funding ‚Üí building
